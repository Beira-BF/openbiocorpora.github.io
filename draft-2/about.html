About Open Biomedical Corpora

The Open Biomedical Corpora (OBC) initative seeks to promote biomedical domain text annotations that are available under Open licenses.

The primary aims of the initiative are to

  1. create a permament online repository of open, manually annotated biomedical domain corpora at http://openbiocorpora.org to preserve the resources and provide unrestricted access to both individual resources and the entire repository.
  2. work with related initiatives, such as BioC and PubAnnotation, to integrate the annotations in terms of abstract representation, data formats, and semantics.

(Current efforts are focusing on the first of these goals.)

Principles

Open annotations

The annotations in OBC resources must be available for anyone to use for any purpose without constraints other than preserving provenance and openness.

That is, the terms under which the annotations are available may require attribution and that derivative works are made available under similar terms (share-alike), but must not impose other constraints.

(See The Open Definition, Definition of Free Cultural Works)

Accessible texts

The texts annotated by OBC resources must be readily accessible to anyone.

Annotated texts should typically be either redistributable or easily available from a persistent online resource.

Manual annotations

The annotations of OBC resources must be in substantial part the outcome of human effort.

(This is a pragmatic constraint: it is comparatively easy to annotate billions of words of text using automatic tools, and including such annotations would strain resources and distract from the focus of the initiative.)

Candidates for OBC principles (for discussion)

Text topics

The texts annotated by OBC resources should mainly be biological, medical, or clinical in their topics. (Precise definition?)

Format

The annotations of OBC resources must be in a format that is well documented (or trivially understandable).

Completeness

The resources included in OBC must be complete. (definition?)

(The repository is not intended to host annotation efforts that are still in progress.)

Documentation

Every set of annotations included in OBC should be described in a peer-reviewed publication or other publicly available document of comparable quality.
